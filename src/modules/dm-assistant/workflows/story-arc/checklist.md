# Story Arc Planning Checklist

## Arc Foundation Validation

### Theme and Stakes
- [ ] Arc has a clear, compelling central theme
- [ ] Stakes are meaningful to all player characters
- [ ] Success and failure outcomes are well-defined
- [ ] Arc theme connects to broader campaign themes
- [ ] Arc length is appropriate for the scope of the story

### Player Character Integration
- [ ] Each PC has a meaningful connection to the arc
- [ ] All PCs have opportunities for spotlight moments
- [ ] PC backstories are incorporated where relevant
- [ ] Character growth opportunities are planned for each PC
- [ ] Arc supports different play styles (combat, exploration, roleplay)

## Story Structure Validation

### Pacing and Flow
- [ ] Arc has clear beginning, middle, and end
- [ ] Session milestones are planned and logical
- [ ] Tension builds appropriately throughout the arc
- [ ] Information reveals are well-timed
- [ ] Each session advances the arc meaningfully

### Flexibility and Adaptation
- [ ] Multiple paths exist to achieve arc objectives
- [ ] Branching points accommodate player agency
- [ ] Contingency plans exist for unexpected player choices
- [ ] Arc can be adapted if pacing needs adjustment
- [ ] Emergency exit strategy exists if arc isn't working

## NPC and World Building Validation

### Supporting Cast
- [ ] Key NPCs have clear motivations and goals
- [ ] NPC relationships with PCs are planned
- [ ] NPCs can grow and change throughout the arc
- [ ] Antagonists have understandable motivations
- [ ] Supporting NPCs serve clear story functions

### World Integration
- [ ] Arc events have logical consequences for the world
- [ ] New locations serve the story and feel authentic
- [ ] World building elements enhance rather than distract
- [ ] Arc creates opportunities for future adventures
- [ ] Cultural and political elements are consistent

## Resource and Logistics Validation

### Preparation Requirements
- [ ] Required NPCs are developed with sufficient detail
- [ ] Key locations are mapped or described adequately
- [ ] Encounters are balanced for the party level and composition
- [ ] Required props, handouts, or resources are prepared
- [ ] Rules references are organized for easy access

### Session Management
- [ ] Each session has clear objectives and potential outcomes
- [ ] Session hooks are engaging and connect to PC motivations
- [ ] Session endings provide natural stopping points
- [ ] Player choice points are identified and planned
- [ ] Backup activities exist in case sessions run short

## Quality Assurance

### Engagement Factors
- [ ] Arc provides variety in encounter types and challenges
- [ ] All players will have opportunities to contribute meaningfully
- [ ] Arc challenges are appropriate for character abilities
- [ ] Story elements will resonate with established player interests
- [ ] Arc avoids common pitfalls that disengage players

### Narrative Coherence
- [ ] Arc events follow logical cause-and-effect relationships
- [ ] Character motivations remain consistent throughout
- [ ] World reactions are proportional and believable
- [ ] Plot threads are properly introduced and resolved
- [ ] Arc conclusion addresses all major story questions

### Campaign Integration
- [ ] Arc enhances established campaign elements
- [ ] Arc doesn't contradict previous events or decisions
- [ ] Arc creates seeds for future storylines
- [ ] Arc rewards reference and build on past player choices
- [ ] Arc advances long-term campaign goals

## Final Review Questions

### Player Agency
- Can players meaningfully affect the arc outcome through their choices?
- Are there multiple valid approaches to achieving arc objectives?
- Will players feel their characters' unique abilities matter to the story?
- Can the arc accommodate unexpected but reasonable player strategies?

### Emotional Resonance
- Will the arc create memorable moments for the players?
- Do the stakes feel personal and meaningful to the characters?
- Will the arc's resolution feel earned and satisfying?
- Does the arc provide opportunities for character growth and development?

### Practical Execution
- Is the arc scope manageable within your preparation time?
- Do you have all necessary resources and materials ready?
- Can you run each planned session confidently?
- Are you prepared to adapt if things don't go as planned?

### Campaign Impact
- Will this arc move the campaign forward in meaningful ways?
- Does this arc create more opportunities than it closes?
- Will the world feel changed by the end of this arc?
- Does this arc set up interesting challenges for the future?

## Arc Readiness Indicators

### Ready to Begin
- [ ] All checklist items above are complete
- [ ] First session is fully prepared with multiple contingencies
- [ ] Key NPCs are developed and ready to roleplay
- [ ] Arc fits naturally into current campaign state
- [ ] You feel confident and excited to run this arc

### Needs More Development
- [ ] Multiple checklist items are incomplete
- [ ] You feel uncertain about key story elements
- [ ] Player character integration feels forced
- [ ] Arc scope feels too large or too small for planned timeframe
- [ ] Required preparation exceeds available time

### Requires Redesign
- [ ] Arc conflicts with established campaign elements
- [ ] Player characters don't connect meaningfully to arc themes
- [ ] Arc scope is inappropriate for campaign level or tone
- [ ] Necessary resources exceed practical limitations
- [ ] Arc premise lacks compelling stakes or clear objectives

Remember: A well-planned arc serves as a framework, not a script. The goal is to create structure that supports great storytelling, not to control every outcome.
